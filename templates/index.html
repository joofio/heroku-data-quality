Concordance Data were considered concordant when there was agreement or compatibility between data elements. This may mean that two elements recording the same information for a single patient have the same value, or that elements recording different information have values that make sense when considered together (eg, biological sex is recorded as female, and procedure is recorded as gynecological examination).
